## THE COMMUNITY ENTERPRISE DOCTRINE

**Three foundations:**

1. **The assets are already there.** Every community has land, skills, natural resources, social structures, local market access, and trust. The enterprise is built on what the community already has â€” not on what it needs to be given.

2. **Collective organisation multiplies individual capacity.** One smallholder farmer has no market power. One hundred organised farmers have a supply contract. The cooperative or community enterprise model converts individual powerlessness into collective market strength.

3. **Wealth stays in the community when the business is owned by the community.** A multinational extracting resources from a community pays wages. A community enterprise extracting the same resources builds community wealth â€” profits recirculate, the community decides how to reinvest, and the enterprise belongs to the people who built it.
